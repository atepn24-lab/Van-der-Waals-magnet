# Van-der-Waals-magnet

MATLAB simulations and analytical modeling of van der Waals bilayer magnets. Includes LLG dynamics, domain walls, interlayer exchange, weakly nonlinear system, Korteweg De-Vries equation, soliton solution and spin-wave excitations.

I share my assistent research experience as a second year electrical and computer engineering student in physics and theoretical engineering. I'm working with two Georgian professors one of them is theoretical engineer and the other is physicist. 

Main subject is nonlinear waves also called solitons in complex physical system - Van der Waals magnet. It is that's given after slightly twisting a two dimensional infinite chromium iodide crystal ferromagnet and letting it find it's ground state. When the ground state establishes we get different crystal domains (mostly rhombohedral, monoclinic). Interesting part is that spin of the magnetization differs between this two domains. One side is ferromagnetic and the other is antiferromagnetic. But in the transition between them we get intresting features. Domain wall (name of the small area where transition happens) hosts the magnons (spin waves). But merely spin waves are not quite interesting and useful, so we seek for the soliton solution, nonlinear wave that is localised due to balance between nonlinearity and dispersion.
